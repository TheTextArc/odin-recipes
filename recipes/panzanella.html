Panzanella

Description:

A salad made with tomatoes, cucumbers, and bread with dressing made from olive oil, lime juice and jalapneos.

Adapted from Leanne Brown's Good and Cheap

Ingredients

  • 2 small field cucumbers or 1 English cucumber, chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 slices of bread
  • Salt and pepper

Dressing

  • 1 jalapeno, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato, chopped
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Optional additions

  • fresh herbs
  • peaches, nectarines, or plums
  • red onion, finely chopped
  • Zucchini or summer squash
  • Olives
  • Grapes

Instructions

  1. If using field cucumbers, peel the skin off since it's tough.
  2. Chop tomatoes, keep 2 tablespoons of the chopped tomatoes for the dressing.
  3. Put the cucumbers and the remaining tomatoes into a large bowl. Sprinkle salt and pepper, then toss the vegetables.
  4. Get a saucepan, put a few drops of olive oil in, then place the saucepan on medium heat.
  5. Saute the jalapeno for 1 minute.
  6. Add in the two tablespoons of tomato set aside for the dressing as well as a tablespoon of water.
  7. Cook for 2 minutes until the tomatoes' juices release. Sprinkle with salt and pepper
  8. After the water evaporates, turn the stovetop off then pour the jalapeno-tomato mixture onto the cutting board.
  9. Chop the mixture finely, then toss back into the pan. Do not put the heat back on.
  10. Put in lime juice and olive oil into the pan. Add salt and pepper as needed.
  11. If the bread is day-old or hard, chop or tear into bite size pieces. Alternatively, toast the bread then tear them up afterwards.
  12. Mix bread and vegetables with the dressing. Apply salt and pepper as needed.
  13. Let the bread soak up the dressing and juices. Afterwads, serve.
